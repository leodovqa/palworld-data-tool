Ultra Teams Guide (2026 Meta)

Bellanoir (Eclipsed Siren — Level 30)

To defeat the standard Bellanoir, you need to account for her status as a "gateway" raid. She has roughly 294,000 HP and is a pure Dark type. Unlike her stronger versions, she does not have a second elemental phase.

The "Standard" Team Composition

Since she is strictly Dark-type, the strategy is straightforward: maximum Dragon damage.

1. The Party (Your Personal Loadout)

  • Mount: Chillet. Its partner skill, Wriggling Weasel, converts all your player damage to Dragon while riding. This is the most efficient way to lower her health personally.
  • Support: 4x Gobfin. Even at low condensation, having four Gobfins in your party provides a significant passive boost to your attack power.
  • Passives for Party: Look for Vanguard (+10% Player Attack) on your Chillet and Gobfins.

2. The Base Army (The 15–20 Pals at your Altar)

Phase Recommended Pals Reason
Full Fight Quivern / Astegon Quivern provides high Dragon DPS and is easy to obtain early. Astegon is even better because its Dark/Dragon typing makes it resistant to Bellanoir’s Dark beams.
Early Game Relaxaurus If you haven't reached the desert/volcano, a team of high-level Relaxaurus with Dragon moves can brute-force the win.

Optimal Passive Skills

  • Musclehead: (+30% Atk) — The best common skill for raw power.
  • Ferocious: (+20% Atk) — Reliable damage boost.
  • Burly Body: (+20% Def) — Important for keeping your base Pals alive during her high-power "Nightmare Beam" attacks.

Bellanoir Libero (Twilight Siren — Level 50)

To defeat the standard Bellanoir Libero, you must prepare for a significant difficulty spike. She has approximately 450,000 HP and features the infamous two-phase typing. She begins as Dark and shifts to Ice for the final 20% of the fight.

The "Libero" Team Composition

You must be ready to switch your elemental focus mid-fight or your Dragon army will be wiped instantly during the Ice phase.

1. The Party (Your Personal Loadout)

  • Mount 1 (Phase 1): Chillet. Use this for the first 80% of the fight to deal Dragon damage.
  • Mount 2 (Phase 2): Ragnahawk. Once she turns blue/white (Ice phase), switch to Ragnahawk to convert your damage to Fire.
  • Support: 4x Gobfin (Condensed). High-star Gobfins are almost mandatory here to ensure you have the DPS to finish the fight before the timer expires.
  • Weapon: Assault Rifle or the Pump-Action Shotgun (Legendary schematic if possible).

2. The Base Army (The 15–20 Pals at your Altar)

Phase Recommended Pals Reason
Phase 1 (Dark) Jetragon / Astegon Jetragon is the highest DPS option. Astegon is the "Tank" option due to its Dark resistance.
Phase 2 (Ice) Jormuntide Ignis He is Fire/Dragon. He resists her Ice attacks and deals massive Fire damage in return.
All-Rounder Blazamut Ryu If you have already beaten the Ryu raid, its hybrid stats allow it to stay in the fight for both phases effectively.

Optimal Passive Skills (2026 Meta)

  • Legend: (+20% Atk, +20% Def, +15% Speed) — Standard for endgame Pals.
  • Musclehead: (+30% Atk) — Essential for the damage race.
  • Serenity: (+10% Atk, -30% Cooldown) — This is the MVP skill for Libero. It allows your Pals to cast Dragon Meteor or Fire Ball significantly more often.
  • Demon God: (+30% Atk, +5% Def) — If you have access to raid-exclusive breeding, this replaces Ferocious.

Pro Strategy: The "Food Buff" Secret

  • Manual Feeding: Don't rely on the feed box during the raid. Manually feed every Pal in your base Mozzarina Cheeseburgers (Atk +20%) right before you summon her. This ensures the buff lasts the entire duration of the fight.
  • Skill Fruit Tip: Give your Fire Pals at least one low-cooldown move (like Ignis Blast). This prevents them from "standing still" while waiting for high-power moves to recharge.

To defeat Bellanoir Libero Ultra in the 2026 meta (v0.7 and beyond), you need to account for her massive HP pool (around 1.2 million) and her unique two-phase typing.

She starts as Dark type but transforms into Ice type when she reaches her final HP threshold (roughly the last 15–20%). This makes standard Dragon teams dangerous because she will shred them in the final phase.

The "Ultra" Team Composition

Since raid bosses now target the player more aggressively, your party should be built around Player Damage Enhancement while your base Pals provide the heavy lifting.

1. The Party (Your Personal Loadout)

  • Mount 1 (Phase 1): Chillet / Chillet Ignis. Riding Chillet converts your player damage to Dragon, which is super effective against her Dark phase.
  • Mount 2 (Phase 2): Ragnahawk / Pyrin. When she turns to Ice, switch to these to convert your damage to Fire.
  • Support: 4x Gobfin (fully condensed). Their "Angry Shark" skill stacks, giving you a massive boost to your own attack power.
  • Passives for Party: Aim for Vanguard and Stronghold Strategist on every Gobfin and mount to buff your own stats.

2. The Base Army (The 15–20 Pals at your Altar)

You need to a distinct groups. Use the Palbox to swap them quickly when the phase changes.

Phase Recommended Pals Reason
Phase 1 (Dark) Jetragon / Astegon Jetragon provides the highest Dragon DPS. Astegon (Dark/Dragon) has the best natural resistance to her Dark beams.
Phase 2 (Ice) Jormuntide Ignis / Blazamut Ryu Jormuntide Ignis is the "MVP" because its Fire/Dragon typing resists her attacks in both phases. Blazamut Ryu is the current DPS king for Fire damage.
All-Rounder Selyne If you don't want to swap, Selyne is the most versatile post-v0.7 Pal for raids due to its high burst magic damage.

Optimal Passive Skills (2026 Meta)

For your Base Pals, move away from old skills like Ferocious. You want the "Big Four" for maximum boss DPS:

  • Legend: (Atk +20%, Def +20%, Move Speed +15%)
  • Demon God: (Atk +30%, Def +5%) — Superior to Ferocious; found on raid-tier Pals.
  • Musclehead: (Atk +30%) — Essential raw damage.
  • Serenity: (Atk +10%, Cooldown -30%) — Crucial. This allows your Pals to spam high-damage moves like Holy Burst or Dragon Meteor much more frequently.

Pro Strategy: The "Three Pillars" of Success

  1. The Food Buff: Feed your base Pals Mozzarina Cheeseburgers before summoning. It grants a 20% attack boost and, more importantly, keeps their hunger from dropping, which prevents a 20% stat penalty mid-fight.
  2. Skill Selection: Ensure your Pals have at least one low-cooldown move (2–4 seconds). If they only have high-power/high-cooldown moves, they will stand idle for 10 seconds after one attack, losing massive DPS.
  3. Base Safety: Build your Summoning Altar on a raised stone platform or in a base with no critical infrastructure. Bellanoir's "Nightmare Ray" will delete chests and crafting stations instantly.
